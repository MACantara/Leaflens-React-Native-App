1. Narra
Scientific name: Pterocarpus indicus
The Narra tree is a tall, sturdy hardwood with golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It grows abundantly in Cavite’s lowland forests and along roadsides. Its wood is prized for furniture making and symbolizes strength and resilience in Filipino culture.
2. Mahogany
Scientific name: Swietenia macrophylla
Mahogany trees have reddish-brown trunks and compound leaves that form wide canopies. They are commonly planted in Cavite’s urban and rural areas for shade and reforestation. Its timber is highly valued for construction and cabinetry.
3. Acacia
Scientific name: Samanea saman
Acacia trees feature a wide, umbrella-like canopy with feathery leaves. They are widespread in Cavite’s open fields and school grounds. The tree provides excellent shade and helps prevent soil erosion.
4. Mango
Scientific name: Mangifera indica
Mango trees are large evergreens with glossy leaves and fragrant yellowish-white flowers. They thrive in Cavite’s warm, dry climate and are often seen in backyards. The fruit is a beloved local delicacy and a major source of income for farmers.
5. Banana
Scientific name: Musa acuminata
Banana plants are tall herbs with broad leaves and hanging clusters of fruit. They grow well in Cavite’s moist soil and humid environment. Bananas are a staple food and used in many Filipino dishes and desserts.
6. Guava
Scientific name: Psidium guajava
Guava trees have smooth bark, oval leaves, and round green fruits rich in vitamin C. They are often cultivated in Cavite’s home gardens. The fruit and leaves are used for food and traditional medicine.
7. Santan
Scientific name: Ixora coccinea
Santan is a small ornamental shrub with clusters of bright red or orange flowers. It decorates many gardens and schoolyards in Cavite. The plant is also used in folk medicine and attracts butterflies.
8. Bougainvillea
Scientific name: Bougainvillea glabra
Bougainvillea is a thorny vine with papery pink or purple bracts. It thrives in Cavite’s sunny climate and is often grown on fences and walls. The plant adds vibrant color to many homes and public spaces.
9. Camia
Scientific name: Hedychium coronarium
Camia is a fragrant white flowering plant with lush green leaves. It grows in shaded gardens and moist soils across Cavite. The flowers are used in leis and local festivities.
10. Rosal
Scientific name: Gardenia jasminoides
Rosal is a bushy shrub with glossy leaves and creamy white, aromatic flowers. It is a favorite ornamental plant in Cavite households. Its beauty symbolizes purity and affection.
11. Calachuchi
Scientific name: Plumeria rubra
Calachuchi trees have thick branches and fragrant, waxy flowers. They grow well in Cavite’s dry soil and are common near churches and cemeteries. The plant is associated with beauty and remembrance.
12. Langka
Scientific name: Artocarpus heterophyllus
Langka, or jackfruit, is a large tree with leathery leaves and huge green fruits. It is widely grown in Cavite’s rural areas. The fruit, seeds, and wood are all useful for food and carpentry.
13. Atis
Scientific name: Annona squamosa
Atis trees are small and bear scaly, sweet fruits. They thrive in Cavite’s warm climate and well-drained soil. The fruit is rich in nutrients and commonly eaten fresh.
14. Papaya
Scientific name: Carica papaya
Papaya is a fast-growing plant with a single trunk and large lobed leaves. It grows in many Cavite gardens and farms. Its fruit is used for food, and its latex is valued in traditional medicine.
15. Coconut
Scientific name: Cocos nucifera
Coconut palms have tall, slender trunks and feathery leaves. They are found in coastal areas of Cavite. Every part of the tree — from fruit to husk — is used in food, oil, and crafts.
16. Talisay
Scientific name: Terminalia catappa
Talisay trees have broad leaves that turn red before falling. They are common in Cavite’s beaches and parks. The nuts are edible, and the leaves are used for natural dye.
17. Molave
Scientific name: Vitex parviflora
Molave trees are medium-sized with hard, durable wood. They grow in Cavite’s dry upland forests. The timber is used in construction and symbolizes endurance.
18. Dapdap
Scientific name: Erythrina variegata
Dapdap trees have red-orange flowers and thorny branches. They are planted in Cavite as ornamental and shade trees. The flowers are believed to bring good luck.
19. Ipil-ipil
Scientific name: Leucaena leucocephala
Ipil-ipil is a fast-growing tree with small, rounded leaves. It is used in Cavite as animal fodder and soil fertilizer. The plant helps restore soil fertility.
20. Banaba
Scientific name: Lagerstroemia speciosa
Banaba is a flowering tree with vibrant purple blossoms. It grows along Cavite’s roadsides and gardens. The leaves are used as herbal medicine for diabetes.
21. Tsaang Gubat
Scientific name: Carmona retusa
Tsaang Gubat is a woody shrub with small, glossy leaves. It thrives in Cavite’s semi-shaded areas. The leaves are brewed as tea for stomach ailments.
22. Makahiya
Scientific name: Mimosa pudica
Makahiya is a low-lying herb whose leaves fold when touched. It grows wildly in Cavite’s grassy fields. It is admired for its unique movement and used in herbal remedies.
23. Adelfa
Scientific name: Nerium oleander
Adelfa is a tall shrub with clusters of pink or white flowers. It grows well in Cavite’s sunny areas. Despite its beauty, it is toxic and should be handled carefully.
24. Kamuning
Scientific name: Murraya paniculata
Kamuning is a small, fragrant shrub with white flowers. It is commonly used as a hedge or ornamental plant in Cavite. Its wood is also used for small carvings.
25. Lagundi
Scientific name: Vitex negundo
Lagundi is a medicinal shrub with green leaves and violet flowers. It is widely grown in Cavite’s gardens and farms. The leaves are known for treating coughs and colds.
26. Balete
Scientific name: Ficus benjamina
Balete is a large tree with hanging aerial roots. It is often seen in old Cavite towns and parks. Locals associate it with folklore and spirits.
27. Ipil
Scientific name: Intsia bijuga
Ipil trees have dark bark and dense foliage. They grow in Cavite’s forested areas. The wood is durable and used in shipbuilding.
28. Bamboo
Scientific name: Bambusoideae
Bamboo is a tall, hollow-stemmed grass that grows in clusters. It thrives in Cavite’s riversides and farms. It’s used for housing, tools, and crafts.
29. Rosas
Scientific name: Rosa spp.
Roses are thorny shrubs with fragrant, colorful blooms. They flourish in Cavite’s cool highlands like Tagaytay. They symbolize love and are used in perfumes.
30. Anahaw
Scientific name: Livistona rotundifolia
Anahaw palms have fan-shaped leaves and slender trunks. They are common in Cavite’s ornamental gardens. The leaves are used for roofing and decoration.
31. Tuba-tuba
Scientific name: Jatropha curcas
Tuba-tuba is a shrub with large green leaves and small flowers. It grows in Cavite’s dry lands. Its seeds can be processed into biodiesel.
32. Lemon Grass
Scientific name: Cymbopogon citratus
Lemongrass is a tall, aromatic grass with thin, sharp leaves. It grows well in Cavite’s gardens and farms. The plant is used for cooking and herbal tea.
33. Saging na Saba
Scientific name: Musa balbisiana
Saba banana plants have large leaves and upright fruit bunches. They are widely cultivated in Cavite for snacks like banana cue and turon. Their leaves are used for wrapping food.
34. Kalamansi
Scientific name: Citrus microcarpa
Kalamansi trees are small with dark green leaves and round fruits. They are common in Cavite backyards. The fruits are used in sauces, drinks, and cleaning.
35. Okra
Scientific name: Abelmoschus esculentus
Okra plants have yellow flowers and green pods. They are often grown in Cavite’s vegetable gardens. The pods are used in soups and stews.
36. Malunggay
Scientific name: Moringa oleifera
Malunggay is a small tree with round leaves and white flowers. It grows easily in Cavite’s climate. The leaves are rich in vitamins and used in dishes and herbal medicine.
37. Talong
Scientific name: Solanum melongena
Talong, or eggplant, is a soft-stemmed plant with purple fruits. It thrives in Cavite’s fertile soil. It’s a staple ingredient in many Filipino dishes.
38. Ampalaya
Scientific name: Momordica charantia
Ampalaya is a climbing vine with lobed leaves and bitter fruits. It grows in Cavite’s gardens and fences. The fruit is valued for its health benefits.
39. Pechay
Scientific name: Brassica rapa
Pechay is a leafy vegetable with smooth green leaves. It grows well in Cavite’s cool areas. It’s used in soups and stir-fried dishes.
40. Sitaw
Scientific name: Vigna unguiculata sesquipedalis
Sitaw is a climbing plant with long green pods. It’s widely cultivated in Cavite’s vegetable farms. It’s a key ingredient in Filipino dishes like adobong sitaw.
41. Kangkong
Scientific name: Ipomoea aquatica
Kangkong is an aquatic vegetable with hollow stems and pointed leaves. It grows in Cavite’s water channels and ponds. It’s used in popular dishes like sinigang.
42. Mustasa
Scientific name: Brassica juncea
Mustasa, or mustard greens, have broad leaves and a spicy flavor. It thrives in Cavite’s highlands. It’s commonly pickled or cooked as side dishes.
43. Alugbati
Scientific name: Basella alba
Alugbati is a climbing vine with fleshy green leaves. It grows abundantly in Cavite’s warm areas. It’s rich in iron and used in healthy soups.
44. Kamote
Scientific name: Ipomoea batatas
Kamote plants have heart-shaped leaves and produce sweet tubers. They are common in Cavite farmlands. Both leaves and roots are edible and nutritious.
45. Gabi
Scientific name: Colocasia esculenta
Gabi is a root crop with large, heart-shaped leaves. It grows in Cavite’s damp fields. Its tubers are used in local dishes like laing and sinigang.
46. Luya
Scientific name: Zingiber officinale
Luya is a rhizome plant with narrow leaves and aromatic roots. It grows well in Cavite’s slightly shaded soil. It’s used for cooking and herbal medicine.
47. Bawang
Scientific name: Allium sativum
Bawang has long leaves and underground bulbs divided into cloves. It’s cultivated in Cavite’s vegetable farms. It’s used in cooking and has medicinal properties.
48. Sibuyas
Scientific name: Allium cepa
Sibuyas plants have green tubular leaves and round bulbs. They are grown in Cavite’s dry fields. They’re a vital ingredient in many Filipino dishes.
49. Mais
Scientific name: Zea mays
Mais is a tall grass with ears containing yellow kernels. It grows in Cavite’s farmlands. The grains are used for food, animal feed, and snacks.
50. Palay
Scientific name: Oryza sativa
Palay is a cereal crop with long stems and golden grains. It is the most important crop cultivated in Cavite’s fields. It provides the staple food — rice — for most Filipinos.
